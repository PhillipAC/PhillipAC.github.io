Stranger Lights

Stranger Lights is what I gave the name of my halloween door. The materials I used to create it using a cardboard box, foil, Maki Maki, Raspberry Pi, Arduino Nano, and an addressable LED ribbon.

The Pressure Plate:

To create a pressure plate, which is hidden under a door mat. I took a cardboard box and cut it into two square pieces. On the bottom of each piece I glued a sheet of aluminum foil. I then placed the two pieces together with the foil side facing inward with some additional cardboard to create a gap so the foil would not touch at rest. One sheet of foil was connected to the ground terminal of the Maki Maki while the other one is wired to the space button. When the Maki Maki detects that the circuit has been completed (by the foil sheets touching) it sends a space signal to the Raspberry Pi.

Raspberry Pi:

The Raspberry Pi controls when the light show will play as well as provides the audio. The application cycles through a list of songs and waits for the space button to be pressed. Once the space button is pressed it stops the current song and begins playing the theme music to Stranger Things. At this time it also sends a signal to the Arduino that it is time to start the light show. While the light show is playing the Raspberry Pi ignores any additional space button presses.

Arduino:

The LEDs that I use could not be controlled straight from the Raspberry Pi so I used an Arduino to interface with the ribbon. When the Arduino is not playing through the light show it alternates orange and green lights. However, when a signal over the usb port is detected the Arduino begins the light show. The instructions the Arduino sends to the LED ribbon and the music that the Raspberry Pi plays are synced up. Once the show is over the Arduino returns to its waiting stage

Final Product:

It is so fun to see the trick or treaters walk up to the house. I generally get an audible gasp when they walk across the pressure plate and are welcomed with a short little show. I look forward to adding more in the future.
