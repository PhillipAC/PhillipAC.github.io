Stranger Lights

This is an updated version of my older project. To see my original write up go HERE.

Stranger Lights is what I gave the name of my halloween door. The materials used to make it are:

  • Arduino (Uno)
  • Addressable LED ribbon (WS2812)
  • PIR Sensor
  • DF Player Mini (with SD card)
  • Speaker

How it works:

The arduino keeps track of the current song being played as it loops through a playlist of MP3s stored on the SD card attached to the DF Player Mini. On a loop the arduino checks the signal pin attached to the PIR Sensor to see if it is high. The PIR sensor sends a high signal to the pin when motion is detected. When the arduino sees a high it stops the currently played song and begins to play the light show's song. It then proceeds to control the LED strip sending different arrangements of lights synchorized to the song being played. When the song is over the arduino tells the DF Player Mini to play the next song in the play list.

When the arduino sees a low signal from the PIR sensor it will check the pin attached to the Busy pin of the DF Player Mini. The DF Player Mini sends a low signal over the busy pin when it is playing a song. When it is no longer playing a song the DF Player Mini sends a high signal. When the arduino sees a high signal from the busy pin it will increment the counter for the current song and tells the DF Player Mini to play that song. When counter is greater than the number of songs on the SD card it loops back to 1. Before checking the busy pin again it will wait 50ms to allow the DF Player Mini to begin to play the next song.

Final Product:

It is so fun to see the trick or treaters walk up to the house. I generally get an audible gasp when they walk across the pressure plate and are welcomed with a short little show. I look forward to adding more in the future.
