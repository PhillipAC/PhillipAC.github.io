Remote Sound

The idea behind the Remote Sound project was to have a device setup to play music for my son to help him go to sleep when he moved into his own room. The original idea was simple hit a button, play music. Around the time I began this project Amazon had a discount on their Dash buttons and I began wondering if I could use the devices with my project to create a remote button.

After a little research I learned that the Dash buttons would turn on and connect to the local network and then send a request to the amazon servers when the button was pressed and then turn off. I decided to write a program to sniff my local network and detect when the amazon button connected at which point the program would start, or stop, playing music.

Eventually I also decided that I would provide the ability to hardwire a button to the Raspberry Pi just in case the remote was lost or we were just in the room. The Git respository for Stranger Lights can be found at https://github.com/PhillipAC/strangerLights
